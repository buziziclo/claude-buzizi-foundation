Logo Lab — Round 2

Six mission-driven marks, all icon-left.

The Rwandan sun is the through-line: 24 rays around a central disc, symbolizing enlightenment, unity, and a new dawn for the nation. I've used it as the anchor and stretched it across six directions — sun + sheltering hand, sun + open palm, sun + sorghum stalk, sun rising over a hill, sun as a community gathering, sun as an open doorway.

Every mark sits left, every wordmark sits right, and the proportions match a real header. Pick the one that lands.

Pure heritage
Option 01

Rwandan Sun

The sun from Rwanda's flag, drawn precisely: 24 rays of equal length around a solid disc. The disc is the only filled element in the mark, painted in your accent gold-orange. Everything else is a hairline.

Why it works: Direct, unambiguous, and instantly readable as Rwandan to anyone from the region. The mission of "enlightenment" is built into the symbol the country chose for itself.

Heritage + mission
Option 02

Sun Over Open Hand

The Rwandan sun above an open palm — the sun gives, the hand catches. Reads as both "a new dawn rises over a community" and "we hold what's given."

Why it works: Two of the most universal symbols of human help, layered. The hand keeps it from being purely national — anyone from anywhere reads "support" before they read "Rwanda."

Place + dawn
Option 03

Dawn Over the Hills

The Rwandan sun rising behind three hills — Rwanda is "the Land of a Thousand Hills." A literal sunrise over the place the story began. Subtitle anchors it to Viscero, Kiwi.

Why it works: Rooted in geography and mission. "A new dawn over the village" is exactly the work. The hills are recognizable to anyone from the region without being a literal map.

Community
Option 04

Gathering Around the Sun

The 24 rays of the Rwandan sun reinterpreted as 8 figures gathered around a central source. The sun feeds, educates, equips — and people circle it. Each figure is a head + body, drawn at the cardinal and ordinal points.

Why it works: Less literal than a flag reference, more about what the sun does. Says "community" before it says "country." Reads as warmth and gathering at any size.

Heritage + harvest
Option 05

Sun and Sorghum

The Rwandan sun in the upper-left, with a stalk of sorghum (the staple grain of Rwanda and East Africa) rising on the right. The grain head is set in your accent color. Two icons of life and sustenance, side by side.

Why it works: Specifically agricultural, specifically Rwandan. Sorghum is what the village eats. The Foundation feeds. The icon already says it before the wordmark does.

Threshold + dawn
Option 06

Doorway to a New Dawn

An arched doorway with the Rwandan sun rising inside it. The doorway is the opportunity the Foundation creates; the sun is what's on the other side. Echoes the architecture of Rwandan rural homes (rounded arched doorways) without copying any one of them.

Why it works: A doorway is the cleanest possible metaphor for "first chance." Combined with the sun, it reads as walking into a new day. Memorable as a silhouette and unique to your foundation.

My recommendation

Option 03 (Dawn Over the Hills) for the brand. Option 01 (Pure Rwandan Sun) as the small-format mark.

Dawn Over the Hills carries the most meaning per stroke: it names where the work began (Rwanda, Land of a Thousand Hills), what the work does (a new day rises), and lets us thread "Viscero · Kiwi · Rwanda" into the wordmark subtitle. The pure sun then becomes your favicon, app icon, and seal — small enough to read at 16px and instantly recognizable as the same brand.
